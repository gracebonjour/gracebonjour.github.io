Advanced Lessons

Level 3 is real-world proofing: trailheads, off-leash parks, wildlife, other dogs. This is also where mistakes get expensive, so don't rush here — see Find a Trainer if you haven't worked with anyone in person yet.

This week's lessons

Lesson 1: Long-line to off-leash

Graduate from a fifty-foot long line to a dragging light line before ever going fully off leash.

Lesson 2: High-value distractions

Squirrels, deer, other dogs — proof your recall against the exact things your dog is most likely to chase.

Lesson 3: Trail reliability

Full sessions on real trails, working up from quiet mid-week mornings to busy weekend afternoons.

Proofing checklist

  1. Confirm a instant recall on a long line around at least three different distraction types.
  2. Drop the line but keep it attached and dragging for several sessions.
  3. Remove the line entirely only in a safe, enclosed area first.
  4. Expand to open trails gradually, always with the e-collar as a backup, not a first response.

When to dial it back

If your dog starts avoiding you, flattening their ears constantly, or hesitating before recalls, that's a sign the stimulation level or your timing needs adjusting — not a sign to push harder. A good reference on reading stress signals is the AVSAB's position statements page linked from the home page.
