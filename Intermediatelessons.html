Intermediate Lessons

Level 2 is about the three Ds: distance, duration, and distraction. You only add one at a time — that's the whole secret.

This week's lessons

Lesson 1: Adding distance

Move the recall practice from three feet to thirty, still indoors or in a quiet fenced yard.

Lesson 2: Duration on a stay

Build a "place" or "stay" command out to two full minutes before you introduce any distraction at all.

Lesson 3: Mild distractions

A bouncing ball, a second person walking by — low-key distractions your dog can realistically succeed against.

Sample training week

Monday & Wednesday
Distance recalls in the backyard, five reps each session.
Tuesday & Thursday
Duration stays with you moving farther away, not adding distraction yet.
Friday
Combine distance and one mild distraction — a good test of where the dog actually is.
Weekend
Rest, or a low-pressure walk with no training goals at all.

Common mistakes

  • Stacking two new variables at once instead of one.
  • Raising the stimulation level to "push through" a distraction instead of lowering criteria.
  • Skipping the reward when the dog does get it right under pressure.
